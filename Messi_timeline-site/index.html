Timeline of Lionel Messi, one of the world's greatest football players. Overview of his life and personal achievements in a beautiful timeline format. View his life timeline.

24 June 1987

Lionel Messi is born

Lionel Andres Messi Cuccittini was born on Wednesday, June 24 1967. He was born in Rosario, Argentina.

31 March 1994

Messi joins Newell's Old Boys

At the age of Messi became a member of the Old Boys. He played for this team for about six years. While he was at the youth club. he scored over 500 goals. The team was almost unbeatable.

2 February 2001

Messi's family moves to Barcelona

Messi and his family relocated to the city of Barcelona in Spain in February 2001. The family moved into an apartment that was very close to Barcelona's football team's stadium, Camp Nou.

17 October 2003

Messi makes his first appearance

The footballer made his first appearance in a match against FC Porto.

25 July 2005

Messi inks his first contract

On his 16th birthday, Messi landed his first contract as a senior team player The contract was completed on June 24, 2005. The contract outlined that he would be an official player with the Barcelona football club until 2010 His buyout clause was set at a whopping €150 million.

18 May 2006

Lionel Messi plays in his first Champions League

Lionel Messi achieves his first champions league on May 17, 2006.

28 May 2009

Messi wins the 2009 UEFA Champions League with Barcelona

The final match of the 2009 UEFA Champions League was played on May 27, 2009. The match was held in Rome, Italy at the Stadio Olimpico. The winner of this match would be crowned the winners of the 2008-09 season of the UEFA Champions League. The winner of the match was Barcelona. They defeated Manchester United 2-0. Lionel Messi scored the final goal of the match. The UEFA Champions is a tournament that involves the top football Clubs in all of Europe.

2 December 2009

Messi earns his first Ballon d'Or

On December l, 2009, Lionel Messi was awarded his very first Ballon d'Or.

11 January 2011

Messi earns his second Ballon d'Or

Messi received his second consecutive Ballon d'Or on January IO, 2011. It was a great year for La Masia, the FC Barcelona academy. It became the first youth academy to have all three of the finalists for the Ballon d'Or in a single year. The players were Lionel Messi, Andres Iniesta, and Xavi.

8 January 2013

Messi wins his Fourth consecutive Ballon d'Or

Messi landed his fourth Ballon d'Or on January 7, 2013. The footballer was, for the fourth consecutive year, named the world's best football player.

7 June 2015

Messi secures Fourth Champions League

On June 6, 2015, Lionel Messi secured his fourth Champions League.

8 March 2017

Messi and Barcelona made huge 6-1 comeback against PSG

FC Barcelona 6–1 Paris Saint-Germain F.C. was the result of the second leg of a UEFA Champions League tie which occurred on 8 March 2017 at the Camp Nou in Barcelona. FC Barcelona overcame a four-goal deficit in the second leg of their 2016–17 UEFA Champions League round of 16 tie against Paris Saint-Germain to win 6–5 on aggregate.

29 September 2020

Messi outshines Cristiano Ronaldo as most marketable athlete

Lionel Messi was declared the Most Marketable Athlete of 2020. His rival, Cristiano Ronaldo finished at the second spot on the list. The two are on the list ahead of other major athletes like Lebron James and Virat Kohli.

5 August 2021

Messi left Barcelona for PSG due to club's bad financial conditions

On one fine evening on August 5, 2021, Barcelona announced via their social media that Lionel Messi was leaving as a free agent and that he couldn't be registered with the league because of their dire economic problems.

11 July 2021

Argentina beat Brazil 1-0 to win Copa America 2021

Argentina won their first maior title in 28 years on Saturday and Lionel Messi finally won his first medal in a blue-and-white shirt when an Angel Di Maria goal gave them a 1-0 win over Brazil and a record-equaling 15th Copa America.

29 November 2021

Messi wins his Seventh Ballon d'Or

Messi, 34, effectively retained the trophy he last won in 2019 — controversially, the award was not handed out by France Football last year because of the coronavirus pandemic — after a year in which he ended his long wait for an international honor, winning the Copa América with Argentina, and left Barcelona, the club where he had spent all of his career, for Paris St.-Germain.

18 December 2022

Messi completes his legacy by winning the World Cup with Argentina

Argentina won the 2022 World Cup final against France, beating the defending champion during a penalty kick shootout after the teams reached full time with a 3-3 tie. The game had headed into 30 extra minutes in a 2-2 tie.
